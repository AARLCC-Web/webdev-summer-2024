About Him

Emridge “E.J.” Jones, Jr. was a gifted musician and mentor born in Chicago, Illinois, on July 10, 1950. Growing up, he displayed a natural talent for music and excelled in playing various instruments like the saxophone, piano, and flute. After completing his education at John Marshall Harlan High School and earning a Bachelor of Science degree in Music Education from Florida Agricultural and Mechanical University in 1972, he furthered his musical studies at Roosevelt University in Chicago. Jones’s musical journey led him to establish the acclaimed contemporary jazz group E.J. and Company, known for their unique and rhythmic sound. Apart from his musical endeavors, Jones was dedicated to nurturing young talents through his roles as a music instructor and band director. His impact extended to the community where he engaged in youth programs and provided mentorship in music and the business aspects of the industry. Despite his untimely passing at the age of 51 due to colon cancer in 2002, Jones’s legacy lives on through the scholarships provided by the foundation established in his honor. His contributions and achievements were recognized through various prestigious awards, highlighting his significant influence in the music scene and his commitment to uplifting young African American artists.
